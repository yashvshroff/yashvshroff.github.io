Activity Involvement at Cupertino High School

Over my last 3.5 years as a Cupertino High School Student I have been involved within numerous extracurricular clubs and teams, however, these are the activities that allowed me to develop fundamental industry skills, giving me an edge against other prospective quants.

  • Cupertino Future Business Leaders of America

    • Competitive Record
      • By competing up to the national level for Cupertino FBLA, I have been able to learn and practice many skills most quantitative analysts sustain.
      • As a Personal Finance competitor, I studied and practiced financial theories, stock market trends, and financial mathematics problems, developing necessary quantitative financial knowledge.
      • As an Organizational Leadership competitor, I practiced and mastered the art of business management, through both the corporate and personal level, all while developing fundamental leadership traits to lead quant discussions and successfully persuade my clients.
      • As a 3D Animation competitor, I was allowed to practice creativity to create financial projection models (as both animation and portfolio development require a creative mindset), and technical development skills. I won 2nd place at SLC 2018 and progressed to NLC in Baltimore, Maryland.
    • American Enterprise Committee Member (2016/2017)
      • I was able to show my mastery of business management skills by participating as a Sedgwick Elementary School Business Teacher. Every year the Cupertino FBLA Chapter sends members to the local elementary school to act as business mentors to the little kids. I was privileged to be a mentor, helping teach a group of ~18 students fundamental management skills. I also got to see the positive effect of our lessons, as I got to see the students I teach successfully start their own business and sell their items to their fellow classmates at their winter marketplace festival.
    • Middle Level Pseudo Bay Section Judge (2016/2017)
    • I also developed analytical skills as I was a judge for a speaking competition at Pseudo-Bays, an FBLA competition conference for local middle schools. My partner and I kept track of competitor’s speeches and evaluated their knowledge and understanding within seconds.
    • California FBLA Financial Literacy Award Recipient (2017/2018/2019)
      • I worked towards and was awarded the California FBLA Financial Literacy award which highlights my mastery of personal finance. This award spurred my interest in finance, as after receiving this honor, I started to read informative economic news articles and analyze stock market trends daily.

    • Professional Technical Experience
      • As an engineer in both the FIRST Tech Challenge and FIRST Robotics Challenge, I have been able to practice my technical quantitative development skills in a more practical sense. Quantitative Analyst Relevant Coursework

During high school, I chose specific classes to further specialize my quantitative analysis skills.

  • Principles of Business

    • This course taught me almost everything there is to know about the field of business. From business management to financial analytics, I have been able to learn about the vast field through many research projects and activities. This class got me interested in Quantitative Analysis after letting me pursue a career research project on it. This class developed my fondness towards business and finance, and taught me most of the management and financial applications all quants must know before joining the industry.

  • AP Computer Science A

    • This course has given me the opportunity to write computer programs and utilize Java algorithm to create unique projects. I was introduced to quantitative programming methods such as Object Oriented Programming and algorithmic problem solving. These programs are especially useful when quants are analyzing thousands of data points from the stock market, and need a fast way to organize their findings.

  • AP Statistics

    • This class was very important in my journey to become a quantitative analyst because statistics is the most common form of quantitative analytics. From creating stock market prediction models using null hypotheses to finding the best investments by analyzing the normality of stock values, statistics is used during most of a quant’s work.

  • AP Calculus AB

    • I learned the fundamentals of integral, differential, and limit calculus; fundamental mathematical theories all quants must utilize while projecting financial models. This class also touched upon the financial application of calculus, through optimization, and allowed me to experience mathematics from a more business perspective.

  • AP Physics C: Mech & AP Physics C: E&M

    • These two advanced courses teach the fundamentals of physics, and explain how everything interacts with each other. Through calculus based physics, I create mathematical models for hundreds of lab data points, and gain meaningful education on how to analyze them. It allows me to practice finding peaks and patterns in lots of data, just like quants do with the stock market.

  • International Business

    • This course allowed me to understand different financial systems across the world. As a quant is not restricted within the United States, this class gave meaningful education about world wide exchanges. My notes document shows my personal research on world-wide affairs of a quantitative analyst.
